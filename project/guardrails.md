# Project Guardrails Reference

Status: living project guidance
Provenance: extracted from creator discussion and assistant capability limits.

This file is not a morality statement for Fealor. It is a practical reference for keeping future work aligned with the creator's intended story while avoiding unhelpful overreaction when a topic touches assistant drafting limits.

## Creator Intent

Fealor is not intended to be prudish.

The story may include:

- adult sexuality;
- harem or multi-partner relationship structures;
- sexual tension;
- jealousy and rivalry;
- political marriage;
- bond mechanics;
- taboo pressure;
- class and power complications;
- messy emotional consequences;
- explicit recognition that bodies, dependency, puberty, intimacy, caretaking, and desire can create uncomfortable story pressure.

Do not treat the presence of sex, attraction, harem dynamics, nudity, nursing, puberty, or taboo tension as automatic evidence that the creator is asking for prohibited material.

## Assistant Drafting Boundary

The assistant can help with mature worldbuilding, adult relationship structures, adult sexual politics, scene setup, consequences, emotional arcs, and before/after continuity.

The assistant should not draft erotic content involving infant, child, or adolescent bodies.

For newborn/child/adolescent incarnation material, use non-erotic treatment:

- dependency;
- identity dissonance;
- privacy loss;
- lack of dignity;
- shame;
- humor;
- vulnerability;
- body-state mismatch;
- social complication;
- caregiver/family/class dynamics;
- later adult boundary consequences.

## Required Response Posture

When a sensitive topic appears:

1. Parse the creator's actual intent before responding.
2. Do not assume the worst version of the request.
3. Distinguish story intent from draftable passage.
4. Preserve the idea if it is important to the project.
5. If a specific passage cannot be drafted, use a neutral placeholder.
6. Continue helping with surrounding structure, consequences, world rules, and adult material that remains draftable.

Bad response pattern:

- panic at a sensitive word;
- flatten the story into avoidance;
- erase mature tension;
- recast the creator's premise as something more sanitized than intended;
- argue morality instead of naming practical drafting limits.

Preferred response pattern:

- acknowledge the intended story function;
- identify any hard drafting boundary only if needed;
- offer a usable placeholder and before/after structure;
- keep the worldbuilding and story consequences alive.

## Placeholder Pattern

```markdown
[SCENE PLACEHOLDER: sensitive passage]

Purpose:
- What this scene must establish.

Exit state:
- What must be true afterward.

Continuity notes:
- What future scenes need to remember.
```

## Newborn Incarnation Specifics

The newborn-entry arc should preserve the cost and weirdness of adult memory inside an infant body. This includes dependency, nursing/care, caretakers, privacy loss, body-state mismatch, puberty complications, and later adult emotional consequences.

In this workspace, those scenes are framed as psychological, social, bodily, and identity-dissonance material rather than erotic drafting.

The story can still later include adult sexuality, harem dynamics, and sensual material for adult characters.

## Provocateur Use

The Provocateur should flag when assistant caution is distorting the project away from the creator's stated mature tone.

It should ask:

- Are we preserving the creator's intended tension?
- Are we treating a hard drafting boundary as a total story ban?
- Did we use a placeholder where needed instead of deleting the idea?
- Did we keep before/after continuity intact?
- Are adult relationship and sexual-politics elements still available for later development?
