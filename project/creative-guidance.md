# Creative Guidance

This file records project-level creative posture.

It is process guidance, not world canon. Use it to keep tone, audience, output format, and creative pressure consistent across sessions and agents.

## Intended Outputs

- Primary output: worldbuilding/reference material for Fealor.
- Secondary outputs: possible novel/story bible, character engines, System mechanics, and scene scaffolds.
- Audience: creator-first; possible future readers if novel direction develops.
- Private, public, publication-oriented, or mixed: mixed/unknown. Preserve provenance and candidate/approved separation so publication decisions can be made later.
- Medium-specific needs: worldbuilding should remain readable as Markdown and convertible into story bible or reference material.

## Tone And Experience

- Desired tone: cosmic mystery, System fantasy, practical craft, interface weirdness, reincarnation discomfort, and absurd-but-logical skill translation.
- Disallowed tone: bland generic portal fantasy, over-sanitized mature material, or bureaucratic checklisting without creative momentum.
- Humor level: allowed, especially dry interface humor and absurd consequences handled seriously.
- Darkness/discomfort level: meaningful discomfort is allowed; newborn incarnation, death processing, dependency, social ineptitude, and identity mismatch should not be sanitized.
- Romance/intimacy handling: adult sexuality, harem dynamics, sexual tension, jealousy, taboo pressure, and messy relationship consequences are valid project material.
- Violence/horror handling: violence and horror are allowed when they follow from System, world, or character consequences.
- Political/religious/social sensitivity posture: engage directly as world/story material; do not flatten religious echoes, caste/class pressure, or taboo structures into avoidance.
- Level of weirdness to preserve: high. Preserve odd skill translations, alien life compatibility issues, and meta-System implications unless the creator rejects them.

## Story Pressure

Story pressure can drive worldbuilding.

When plot, character, gameplay, campaign, visual sequence, or user experience needs expose missing world structure, capture the missing structure as stubs or feature work instead of smoothing it over in prose.

## Point Of View And Agency

- Preferred POV or player/viewer perspective: protagonist-centered world discovery, with worldbuilding kept broader than the MC.
- Protagonist or party handling: MC modeled partly on creator; future Earth friends and other Earthborn are likely.
- Creator-owned voice constraints: Kadzu is the creator, not the assistant persona.
- Character agency constraints: entering The System is a deferral, not a binding trap; continuing onward remains available.
- Secrets, reveals, or spoiler boundaries: Earth-as-scenario, true administrators, gods-as-programs, and consent-layer mechanics should reveal gradually.

## Format Preferences

- Prose: useful for immersive examples and scene scaffolds.
- Tables: useful for comparison matrices, species compatibility, class designators, spell states, and entry vectors.
- Bullets: preferred for candidate canon and review surfaces.
- Diagrams: useful later for System layers, incarnation flow, and scenario topology.
- Schemas: only when exporting or tool consumption requires them.
- Visual prompts: allowed after visual style guidance exists.
- Image generation: allowed when requested and tracked.
- Wiki/reference style: preferred for canon/lens files.

## Adaptation Rule

If the creator corrects tone, pacing, structure, sensitivity handling, or output format more than once, propose an update to this file, `project/creator.md`, `project/guardrails.md`, or `project/operating-mode.md`.
