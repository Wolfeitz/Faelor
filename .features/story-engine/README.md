## Current Story Shape

The main character is modeled partly on the creator: an experienced programmer, AI/prompt specialist, worldbuilder, homelab operator, workflow builder, image-restoration toolmaker, gamer, fantasy-audiobook listener, craft hobbyist, and keeper of several oddly useful real-world skill clusters.

The MC enters a new magic-fantasy System world as a newborn after discovering that newborn incarnation is a hidden, statistically disfavored option. This choice delays physical agency but creates years of uninterrupted System-interface experimentation.

## Defining MC Advantage

The MC is not special because the System breaks for him. He is special because he recognizes the System as an interface with hidden defaults, recommendation layers, guardrails, query sensitivity, and composable primitives.

Core line:

> The System is not broken by better questions. It is revealed by them.

Related principle:

> The default interface is not a prison. It is a convenience layer. The problem is that almost everyone mistakes convenience for reality.

The MC's early advantage is power-user behavior:

- asking better questions;
- exposing hidden menu options;
- requesting definitions and assumptions;
- separating advisory defaults from hard rules;
- building private notes, query templates, and indexes;
- learning to compose System primitives in unintended but valid ways.

## Newborn Incarnation Strategy

Newborn incarnation appears unattractive to most entrants because it removes immediate agency, locks most skills, risks memory suppression, and produces years of helplessness. For this MC, it creates a unique research window.

During infancy and childhood he can do little physically, but he can think, query, observe, index, and gradually build an internal learning architecture.

Potential hidden-entry options:

- Native Birth.
- Early Recall.
- Adolescent Emergence.
- Adult Arrival.
- Vessel Inheritance.
- Constructed Body.
- Lineage Integration.
- Ancestral Echo.
- Summoned Outsider.
- Dream Incursion.
- Dungeon-Bound Core.
- Familiar/Companion Vector.
- Deity-Touched Incarnation: unavailable or locked.
- Administrative Observer: locked.
- Worldseed Participant: criteria unknown.

## Sensitive Thread: Newborn Incarnation And Caregiver Intimacy

Purpose:

- Explore the psychological cost of adult memory in an infant body.
- Include dependency, privacy loss, body-state mismatch, caregiver intimacy, shame, humor, vulnerability, puberty complications, and later adult boundary problems.
- Preserve the consequences of newborn incarnation instead of pretending they do not exist.

Treatment in this workspace:

- Infant, child, and adolescent scenes are dependency, identity-dissonance, vulnerability, and social-complication scenes.
- Do not draft erotic content involving an infant, child, or adolescent body in this workspace.
- When a scene requires material beyond the assistant's boundaries, mark it with a neutral placeholder, preserve narrative purpose and exit state, and continue with before/after story structure.
- Adult romance, harem structures, sexuality, jealousy, political marriage, bond mechanics, and sensual tension can be developed later for adult characters.

## Earth Friends Hook

The MC may eventually discover or build a narrow outbound channel to Earth or to the death-interface waiting room.

Potential message modes:

- dreams or intrusive thoughts;
- AI/chat/log artifacts;
- forum posts or story fragments;
- probability nudges;
- dead-man packets hidden in prompts;
- posthumous messages delivered when a friend reaches their own death interface.

Cliffhanger possibility:

An Earth friend eventually arrives in the same world and says, "You absolute bastard. I got your message."

This also enables deliberate skill preloading: friends who believe the message can train strange Earth skills before entry, creating future entrants with odd but valid translation profiles.
