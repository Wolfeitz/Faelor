# Incarnation Vectors

Status: candidate canon
Provenance: generated from creator direction and current Fealor System model.

## Core Principle

An incarnation vector is the method by which a consciousness enters a scenario world.

The default interface presents the safest, most common, most narratively legible options first. Other vectors exist, but may be hidden because they are statistically disfavored, psychologically difficult, expensive, risky, boring to external observers, or outside the standard onboarding flow.

Incarnation vector determines:

- starting age;
- body continuity;
- memory continuity;
- cultural integration;
- legal/social identity;
- skill integration;
- class/profession timing;
- vulnerability window;
- long-term growth potential;
- how much local reality accepts the entrant as native.

## Standard Entry Flow

Most entrants experience a polished sequence:

1. stabilize after death;
2. receive short explanation;
3. select broad scenario category;
4. select recommended world or curated options;
5. choose standard entry package;
6. choose or confirm memory settings;
7. receive class/profession guidance;
8. enter.

The default path is not a prison. It is a convenience layer.

The MC breaks out of this flow by asking about hidden vectors, non-recommended options, older interfaces, detailed configuration modules, and lower-level access.

## Age And Integration Rule

Age controls access. Class controls direction. Experience controls available material.

Prior experience cannot fully integrate unless the current incarnation has compatible:

- neural development;
- body mechanics;
- mana channels;
- soul capacity;
- language/culture anchors;
- local-world identity;
- class or profession vectors;
- scenario permissions.

Locked experience is recognized but unavailable until unlock criteria are met.

## Candidate Vectors

### Native Birth

Description:

The entrant is born naturally into the target world.

Advantages:

- deepest local integration;
- strongest long-term growth;
- native family, language, culture, and legal identity;
- least obvious foreignness;
- best compatibility with local body/soul/mana development.

Costs:

- years of helplessness;
- most skills locked;
- memory continuity may be suppressed, unstable, or filtered;
- dependency on family/caretakers;
- vulnerability to childhood disease, politics, neglect, abuse, or accident;
- no immediate agency.

Likely uptake:

Extremely low among entrants who retain enough selfhood to choose knowingly.

### Early Recall

Description:

The entrant begins as a child or young native with partial memory returning early.

Advantages:

- strong cultural integration;
- some early agency;
- faster story activation than native birth;
- easier to hide unusualness than adult arrival.

Costs:

- partial memory confusion;
- family and social obligations already exist;
- child body limits;
- risk of being seen as possessed, blessed, cursed, or strange.

### Adolescent Emergence

Description:

The entrant awakens or fully recalls during adolescence.

Advantages:

- balance of agency and integration;
- class/profession selection window may still be open;
- physical growth and training can still shape build;
- strong coming-of-age story pressure.

Costs:

- identity collision with existing adolescence;
- emotional/body instability;
- social expectations already forming;
- prior skills partially locked or distorted.

### Adult Arrival

Description:

The entrant appears as an adult, usually through constructed body, outsider arrival, summoning, or scenario insertion.

Advantages:

- immediate agency;
- stronger prior identity continuity;
- more mental skills available quickly;
- fast adventure start.

Costs:

- weaker cultural integration;
- body mismatch;
- obvious foreignness;
- reduced long-term plasticity;
- less local family/legal embedding.

### Vessel Inheritance

Description:

The entrant occupies or merges with a recently vacated compatible body.

Advantages:

- immediate body, history, and social position;
- built-in plot and obligations;
- local language/body habits may be partially available;
- less conspicuous than appearing from nowhere.

Costs:

- moral ambiguity;
- inherited enemies, debts, relationships, and trauma;
- identity conflict with body's prior life;
- possible haunting, rejection, or memory contamination.

### Constructed Body

Description:

The entrant receives a custom body generated for the scenario.

Advantages:

- configurable traits;
- fewer family constraints;
- possible optimization;
- clean separation from prior local history.

Costs:

- expensive or restricted;
- may lack deep world anchoring;
- may attract attention;
- may come with artificial-body limitations or maintenance needs.

### Lineage Integration

Description:

The entrant is inserted into a family line, bloodline, clan, caste, or inherited role.

Advantages:

- strong social anchoring;
- access to lineage abilities, obligations, or protections;
- plausible local identity.

Costs:

- inherited politics;
- bloodline expectations;
- family control;
- compatibility constraints.

### Ancestral Echo

Description:

The entrant enters as a returning ancestor-pattern, reincarnation, or echo within an existing lineage.

Advantages:

- mythic legitimacy;
- title/legacy resonance;
- possible access to ancestral memory structures.

Costs:

- religious scrutiny;
- pressure to fulfill old roles;
- confusion between entrant identity and ancestor identity.

### Summoned Outsider

Description:

The entrant arrives through local summoning, ritual, divine intervention, accident, or System-compatible call.

Advantages:

- immediate story hook;
- local actors expect an outsider;
- possible patron, contract, or summoner support.

Costs:

- obligations;
- contracts;
- political or magical ownership claims;
- unstable trust.

### Dream Incursion

Description:

The entrant enters indirectly through dreams, possession-like projection, avatar states, or intermittent embodiment.

Advantages:

- lower commitment;
- reconnaissance;
- unusual information access.

Costs:

- unstable agency;
- unclear identity;
- limited physical interaction;
- high metaphysical risk.

### Dungeon-Bound Core

Description:

The entrant incarnates as, inside, or bound to a dungeon core, territory core, worldseed, or similar control node.

Advantages:

- infrastructure control;
- growth through environment;
- direct tie to System mechanics.

Costs:

- immobility;
- predation by adventurers, gods, or administrators;
- severe social isolation;
- high attention.

### Familiar / Companion Vector

Description:

The entrant enters as a bonded companion, familiar, guide spirit, artifact intelligence, or secondary consciousness.

Advantages:

- immediate relationship anchor;
- unusual perspective;
- access to bond mechanics.

Costs:

- dependency;
- reduced autonomy;
- ownership/bondage risks;
- hard path to independent agency.

### Deity-Touched Incarnation

Description:

The entrant enters under a god, divine program, or story-arc layer.

Advantages:

- patronage;
- title resonance;
- narrative protection or pressure;
- access to divine quests.

Costs:

- surveillance;
- obligations;
- manipulation by god-layer systems;
- conflict with true administrators.

Status:

Likely locked, restricted, or high-risk.

### Administrative Observer

Description:

The entrant enters with observer, moderator, tester, or administrator-adjacent permissions.

Advantages:

- unusual System visibility;
- potential access to rule layers;
- relevance to locked World Administration experience.

Costs:

- likely unavailable;
- dangerous attention;
- severe restrictions;
- risk of damaging self/world configuration.

Status:

Locked or criteria unknown.

### Worldseed Participant

Description:

The entrant enters a new or developing world with a role tied to shaping, seeding, testing, or stabilizing that world.

Advantages:

- high growth potential;
- direct relevance to worldbuilding, workshop, and System architecture skills;
- possible path toward higher authority.

Costs:

- unstable scenario;
- poorly documented rules;
- high responsibility;
- attention from gods/admins/watchers.

Status:

Criteria unknown.

## Newborn Entry

The MC chooses Native Birth / Newborn Entry after discovering hidden options.

Why it works for him:

- he has Promptcraft;
- he recognizes the interface as configurable;
- he is willing to ask for statistically disfavored options;
- he values long-term compounding advantage;
- his prior experience includes systems, tools, AI, indexing, workflows, and self-directed study;
- years of physical helplessness become years of System-interface research.

Most entrants reject it because:

- it looks boring;
- it delays agency;
- it risks memory suppression;
- it creates total dependency;
- it may be psychologically unbearable;
- it offers no immediate power fantasy;
- the standard interface does not recommend it.

## Infant Access Model

During infancy, active physical and magical expression is severely constrained.

Available or partially available:

- passive memory continuity;
- basic System queries;
- notification settings;
- personal notes;
- observation capture;
- language exposure tracking;
- early Inner Workshop foundations;
- locked-experience review;
- simple indexing/tagging;
- comfort/self-regulation routines.

Unavailable or mostly locked:

- combat skills;
- mature body skills;
- most magic;
- class advancement;
- profession practice;
- adult social agency;
- advanced crafting;
- direct System commands that require mature cognition/body/mana capacity.

Risk:

The infant brain/body may distort memory, emotion, attention, and identity. Adult continuity survives, but not cleanly.

## Memory Retention Options

Possible explanations for the MC's unusual retention:

1. **Selected Continuity**
   He explicitly asks for memory continuity and accepts the risks.

2. **Promptcraft Negotiation**
   He asks the right questions and discovers continuity settings that the standard interface hides.

3. **Prior Consent Layer**
   His consent/resonance to interception included unusual continuity permissions.

4. **Locked Worldbuilder Pattern**
   Worldbuilding/System architecture experience causes the System to preserve continuity for compatibility with hidden future paths.

5. **New World Exception**
   The target world is new or unstable, so its normal memory filters are incomplete.

6. **Administrative Anomaly**
   His MUD admin / System architect / Lughbrugh patterns create an authority-shaped anomaly that prevents ordinary suppression.

Recommended current stance:

Keep the exact cause unresolved at first. The MC can believe he negotiated it, while later evidence suggests more than one factor was involved.

## Costs Of Newborn Entry

Newborn entry must remain costly.

Costs:

- years of dependency;
- no privacy;
- body-state mismatch;
- caregiver intimacy and social complications;
- inability to communicate clearly;
- risk of being discovered as strange;
- infant cognition pressure;
- emotional regression or bleed-through;
- local family obligations;
- vulnerability to disease, violence, neglect, politics, and accident;
- locked skills causing frustration;
- possible System attention from unusual interface activity.

Compensation:

- deep local integration;
- long-term growth runway;
- native language/culture acquisition;
- early knowledge repository;
- years of Promptcraft practice;
- chance to build Inner Workshop before ordinary class pressure begins.

## Provocateur Pass

- Assumptions to confirm:
  - Newborn entry is available in the target world.
  - The MC can preserve enough adult continuity to use the System interface.
  - Infant System access is limited but real.
- Possible conflicts:
  - Too much infant access can erase the cost of newborn entry.
  - Too little access makes the central strategy fail.
  - Memory retention needs mystery without feeling arbitrary.
- Missing decisions:
  - Does the MC explicitly choose memory continuity?
  - Does the System warn him about infant cognition distortion?
  - Who, if anyone, notices his unusual early System activity?
- Drift risks:
  - Turning newborn entry into pure optimization.
  - Letting the MC solve too much before he can act.
  - Making hidden options feel like cheats instead of disfavored legitimate configurations.
- Useful pressure tests:
  - What can he do at one month, six months, one year, three years, seven years?
  - What is the first thing he misunderstands because infant cognition distorts it?
  - What is the first cost he did not anticipate?
