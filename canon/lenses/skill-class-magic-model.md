# Skill, Class, Title, And Magic Model

Status: candidate canon
Provenance: generated from creator direction.

## Core Principle

People are not boxed by class.

Anyone can learn any skill, spell, job, weapon, craft, or discipline they are capable of learning. Class does not determine what a person may do. Instead, accumulated capabilities determine which class designators become available.

Class is a System-recognized growth-vector, label, synthesis path, and advancement bias.

Skills, spells, jobs, titles, attributes, behavior, and accomplishments create the menu of available class designators.

## Class Designators

A class designator is an available System classification based on what a person can already do and what patterns they have demonstrated.

Example:

Someone who knows:

- Spark;
- Light;
- sword combat;
- medium armor;
- pickpocketing;
- lock picking;
- stealth basics;

may have class designators such as:

- Fighter;
- Thief;
- Scout;
- Duelist;
- Spellblade;
- Locksmith;
- Trickster;
- Nightblade;

The designator does not reveal the whole person. A skilled pickpocket can join a party as a Fighter and still steal from them if they choose. A "Thief" class is not required to steal.

## Why Choose A Class?

Choosing a class shapes growth.

Classes can affect:

- which skills improve more efficiently;
- which skills resonate together;
- which spells evolve in class-compatible directions;
- which titles become easier to earn;
- which professions synergize;
- which quests or teachers recognize the person;
- how the System presents recommendations;
- which higher-tier class evolutions become visible;
- how disparate skills combine into techniques.

Classes should be useful, but never totalizing.

## Magic Overview

Magic is not yet divided into fixed schools.

The world may have schools, traditions, guild taxonomies, religious categories, or System labels, but those may be local interpretations rather than hard ontology.

Working principle:

Magic can be learned, practiced, personalized, evolved, combined, and misunderstood.

## Spells

A spell is a repeatable magical pattern.

It may include:

- mana movement;
- intent;
- concept;
- gesture;
- word;
- rune;
- focus;
- breath;
- visualization;
- material component;
- class resonance;
- System-recognized structure.

Spells improve with use.

Improvement may include:

- stronger output;
- lower cost;
- faster casting;
- better control;
- wider range;
- finer targeting;
- reduced backlash;
- altered shape;
- new modes;
- class-compatible evolution;
- personal variants.

## Spell Personalization

Spells adapt to the user over time.

A person who repeatedly uses a spell in unusual, creative, stressful, or highly intentional ways may develop personalized variants.

Examples:

- Spark becomes a precise ignition tool for one caster.
- Spark becomes a combat snap-flash for another.
- Spark becomes a diagnostic current for a runewright.
- Light becomes illumination, signaling, illusion correction, image restoration, or holy-seeming radiance depending on use.

Personalization requirements may include:

- repeated use;
- comprehension;
- emotional imprint;
- class resonance;
- environment;
- intent clarity;
- experimentation;
- stress;
- titles;
- unusual prior experience.

## Spell Mastery States

Candidate states:

- Known: the caster can produce the spell under favorable conditions.
- Practiced: the spell is reliable in ordinary use.
- Fluent: the caster can adjust the spell in real time.
- Personalized: the spell has adapted to the caster.
- Integrated: the spell synergizes with class, skills, or other spells.
- Mastered: the caster can use it under pressure with precision.
- Transcendent: the spell has evolved beyond its original form or become a seed for new magic.

## Synergistic Casting

Spell combination is not simply casting two spells at once.

The deeper mechanic is synergistic casting: creating a combined magical expression from multiple known patterns.

Examples:

- Fire + Water -> steam, scalding mist, pressure burst, cleansing vapor.
- Fire + Air -> furnace blast, directed flame, flash expansion, heat lance.
- Light + Air -> shimmer veil, signal flare, mirage distortion.
- Spark + Metalwork -> charged inscription, ignition rune, diagnostic pulse.
- Water + Earth -> mud binding, clay shaping, mineral slurry.
- Light + Restoration -> image recovery, illusion correction, memory-echo reveal.

Synergistic casting may produce:

- a temporary combined effect;
- a formal compound spell;
- a new personalized spell;
- a class technique;
- a ritual pattern;
- an enchantment procedure.

## Requirements For Synergistic Casting

Possible requirements:

- sufficient mana capacity;
- high magic comprehension;
- strong understanding of each component spell;
- practical experience with each spell;
- conceptual understanding of the interaction;
- mental flexibility;
- class or title resonance;
- safe casting structure;
- possibly attributes such as intelligence, wisdom, will, perception, focus, or equivalent local traits.

The exact attribute model is not settled.

## Beyond Dual Casting

Dual casting may exist as a visible or beginner interpretation of combination magic, but it is not the whole field.

Combination can happen through:

- simultaneous casting;
- staged casting;
- layered casting;
- ritual sequencing;
- prepared matrices;
- runic composition;
- spell braiding;
- class techniques;
- group casting;
- stored spell components;
- Lenses that help visualize compatibility;
- Inner Workshop simulation.

Higher-order casting may combine more than two effects, but complexity and failure risk grow rapidly.

The System may recognize:

- individual spells;

## Provocateur Pass

- Assumptions to confirm:
  - Skills and spells are learnable outside class.
  - Class is selected from available designators, not assigned as a cage.
  - Spell schools are not fixed yet.
  - Spell evolution is use-driven and personalization-friendly.
- Possible conflicts:
  - If anyone can learn anything, social barriers and time costs must matter.
  - Class designators need benefits strong enough to matter without becoming restrictive.
  - Synergistic casting can become too powerful if prerequisites are vague.
- Missing decisions:
  - Are classes chosen once, changeable, stackable, or evolvable?
  - Are class designators public, private, or selectively visible?
  - What attribute model, if any, exists?
  - Who teaches spell theory?
  - Are spell names System-native, cultural, or user-generated?
- Drift risks:
  - Creating generic RPG schools too early.
  - Making "Thief" a moral identity instead of a capability vector.
  - Letting MC knowledge flatten local magical traditions.
- Useful pressure tests:
  - What stops a noble from buying every skill teacher?
  - What stops a thief from calling themselves a fighter?
  - What happens when local guilds disagree with System designators?
  - Can a spell personalize in a harmful direction?
  - Can combined spells become illegal?
