# Cosmic System Model

Status: candidate canon
Provenance: extracted from creator answers during Cosmic/System review.

## Death Processing Space

At death, consciousness is pulled to an in-between processing space tied to the moon array and The System.

This space can play against religious ideas of limbo, bardo, purgatory, the threshold, or the pause between life and whatever comes next. The System can acknowledge the resemblance with dry humor:

> One mystery solved: yes, this is the opportunity to pause before proceeding.

The pause can last as long as the entrant chooses, or at least long enough to make scenario entry decisions. The System still does not claim to know or disclose what naturally comes next.

## Continue Onward

"Continue onward" means releasing the consciousness back into the natural post-death process.

No one in the interface layer actually knows what that means in final terms. Like religions, the System and its operators may have models, theories, doctrines, statistics, or guesses, but they do not possess confirmed knowledge of the ultimate destination or nature of what follows.

The entrant cannot refuse both choices in a meaningful way. The natural process has already been interrupted by interception. From the processing space, the available paths are:

- enter The System for a time; or
- continue on the natural path.

Entering The System is not binding. It does not permanently prevent the entrant from continuing onward later. The entrant may choose to leave The System and proceed whenever permitted by the applicable interface/process rules.

This makes System entry a deferral, pause, detour, or extension rather than salvation, damnation, imprisonment, or final reincarnation.

## Contact Layer

For now, the entrant only directly contacts an AI construct or interface intelligence.

The construct can answer loosely about extraterrestrial races or higher civilizations that maintain consciousness-gathering arrays, but direct contact with builders, operators, gods, or true administrators is not expected early.

## Death-Interface Tone

The default death-interface construct uses triage plus translation.

The entrant has just died. Their consciousness may be unstable, terrified, euphoric, angry, grieving, religiously primed, confused, or dissociated. The first task is orientation, not exhaustive explanation.

Default approach:

1. Stabilize the entrant with an environment their mind can tolerate: light, quiet, a room, a shoreline, a childhood kitchen, a temple threshold, a hospital garden, a blank white interface, or another accepted frame.
2. Avoid pretending to be a specific loved one or deity unless deeper protocol allows it. Culturally legible symbols are acceptable, but the construct should identify itself as guide/interface when asked.
3. Give the shortest true explanation first:

   > You have died. This is an interval before whatever comes next. You may continue onward, or you may pause that passage by entering a structured world. I can explain both choices.

4. Use layered disclosure. Simple first; deeper if the entrant asks.
5. Preserve agency. Continuing onward is not punishment. Entering The System is not salvation.
6. Adapt language to the entrant: religious threshold, scenario menu, substrate continuity, child-safe metaphor, game interface, scientific frame, or other useful translation.
7. Distinguish recommendation from requirement when asked.
8. Let precise questions unlock precise answers within disclosure limits.

The default interaction can be beautiful, comforting, awe-inspiring, familiar, or perfectly reassuring and still remain an interface. The MC's Promptcraft starts to matter when he notices the experience has assumptions and asks questions outside its expected flow.

Unsettling truth:

> The interface can care about your distress without caring about you as a person.

Alternative phrasing:

> It is kind because kindness works. Whether that counts as kindness is left to the dead.

## Array Maintainers

The moon array is likely maintained by extraterrestrial races or higher civilizations, but "core systems" is probably the wrong frame because the array network spans galaxies.

Open terms:

- array maintainers;
- array custodians;
- galactic infrastructure keepers;
- consciousness-network operators;
- higher-system civilizations.

## Gods, Programs, And Administrators

The System appears layered.

Possible layers:

- scenario-facing AI constructs;
- quest and prize systems;
- gods tied to story-arc creation, steering, farming, simulation, or world management;
- administrators above the gods who interact with the overall System;
- true administrators who effectively rule the whole structure.

Gods may be less "ultimate beings" and more like another program, subsystem, or game layer. They may be playing or running a sim/farming-style world game, whether knowingly or not.

True administrators sit above that layer. A major hidden truth may be that new administrators can occasionally be raised from participants who level the relevant skills, titles, and authorities far enough.

## Earth Clues

There should be early clues that Earth may be a scenario or may have System entanglement, but nothing the MC can reasonably assemble into the truth until later.

The clue pattern should feel fair in retrospect, not obvious in the moment.

## Standard Entry Program

The death interface is like being placed into a refined, running program on a computer.

It presents the clean, easy, safe path that has been improved over millennia:

- recommended options;
- popular choices;
- low-risk defaults;
- ordinary scenario selection;
- standard character creation;
- streamlined consent and disclosure;
- "what everyone wants" according to long usage patterns.

The interface mostly does not hard-restrict customization. It leads entrants down the standard user experience.

## Breaking Out Of The Standard Experience

The MC does not exploit the System in the sense of breaking rules. He disrupts the standardized user experience by asking questions outside the current program's expected flow.

Analogy:

> "Yes, I want to customize my burrito, but before we do that, can you explain quantum theory?"

This is not strictly an exploit. It is an off-script query that reveals the interface is only one program among many.

Escalation layers:

1. Standard entry program.
2. Advanced/custom options.
3. Older versions and alternate interfaces.
4. Specialized utilities.
5. Plug-in modules such as detailed character creation.
6. Lower-level command interface where powerful operations are possible but dangerous.

The command layer is where things get real. It requires learning syntax, constraints, and consequences, because a bad operation can damage the entrant's configuration, build, memory, or future integration.
